ABOUT
who are we?
BigRed//Hacks is the first ever student-run, large-scale hackathon at Cornell University. Across three days, students from all across the northeast will gather to build the coolest and most innovative projects, meet amazing people, and win some prizes along the way! We encourage all developers, regardless of technical ability or hackathon experience, to apply! Admission and food is free for all hackers.
FAQ
ask us anything
Who is eligible to participate?
We are inviting all undergraduate and graduate students to participate in our hackathon. You don't need any past experience. You don't even need to be a computer science major! Our only requirement is that you are at least 18 years old and enrolled in an accredited college or university.

What will I need to bring?
Bring your school ID for registration. You are also responsible for bringing any equipment for building and demoing your hack: laptop, chargers, phones, etc. You may also want to bring a sleeping bag, change of clothes, and toothbrush.

Do I have to sign up with a team?
You don't have to signup with a team, but it's certainly more fun! We allow teams of up to four people (they don't have to be from the same school), and while they can specified on the application, you won't officially create your team until the event. If you don't have a team yet, we'll host a social before the hacking begins for you to meet other amazing people.

Are you providing food?
Don't worry, there will be lots of food and drinks (including energy drinks) at our event, free of charge to all participants. We'll have vegetarian options as well.

What's the deal with transportation?
We will reimburse transportation for students up to $150 as needed. There will be a reimbursement table: bring your travel ticket, receipt, and government-issued photo ID. We ask that you pick a reasonably priced method of travel. If you plan on driving, then you will have to find parking outside of the campus. Our goal is to charter buses for popular locations.

What's the schedule for the hackathon?
The following schedule is very tentative and subject to change. As we get nearer to the event, we will post a more complete and accurate schedule which will include food and tech talks. Please schedule your method of travel so that you don't miss anything!

Sept. 26, 5:00PM: Registration Opens
Sept. 26, 6:00PM: Opening Event and API Demos
Sept. 26, 8:00PM: Hacking Begins
Sept. 28, 8:00AM: Hacking Ends
Sept. 28, 9:00AM: Project Exposition
Sept. 28, 12:00PM-2:30PM: Final Presentations and Awards
Are hardware hacks allowed?
While we are allowing all kinds of hacks, including hardware hacks, we currently don't have the means to provide dedicated support for hardware hackers. Hackers will be responsible for bringing any components they wish to use, and won't be provided special tools or space. Any free hardware that sponsors bring will be at their own discretion and will not be handled by us.

What are the rules?
We want BigRed//Hacks to be fun and fair for everyone. Your project should be started from scratch and worked on only during the 36 hours, and you may only incorporate outside resources (code, graphics, sound, etc) that are freely licensed and accessible to all participants equally. Of course, maintain a positive, respectful attitude to your fellow hackers throughout the event.

Why is there an application?
We want to guarantee that students from a wide range of schools, backgrounds, and experiences are given an equal and fair chance to participate. Further, we hope that the process will encourage registrants to be committed to build something awesome. Even if you've never been to a hackathon before, please apply!
